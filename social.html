Social Service

Serving our community through compassion, solidarity, and action

Our Commitment to Community Service

At BRSU, we believe that social service is an essential expression of our values and a powerful way to strengthen our community. Our student-led social service initiatives address urgent needs while building solidarity, resilience, and hope among Rohingya refugees.

Through our community service programs, we aim to improve living conditions, enhance well-being, and create opportunities for Rohingya refugees to support one another. Our approach emphasizes dignity, community participation, and sustainable solutions that empower rather than create dependency.

BRSU's social service work is guided by the principle that those closest to the challenges often have the best insights into effective solutions. We mobilize Rohingya youth volunteers to lead service initiatives, working in partnership with community members, local organizations, and international humanitarian agencies.

Our Service Initiatives

Health & Wellness

Our health initiatives include community health education, mental health support groups, and assistance accessing medical services. We train youth health advocates who provide information on disease prevention, nutrition, and hygiene practices.

Child & Youth Support

We provide safe spaces, recreational activities, and mentorship for children and youth. Our programs include sports clubs, arts activities, and peer support groups designed to promote resilience, social skills, and emotional well-being.

Community Outreach

Our outreach teams identify vulnerable individuals and families, connect them with resources, and provide direct assistance. We conduct regular community needs assessments to ensure our services address the most pressing concerns.

Emergency Response

Our emergency response team mobilizes quickly during crises such as fires, floods, and cyclones. We provide immediate assistance, coordinate with relief agencies, and help affected families access emergency services and supplies.

Elder Care & Support

We provide companionship, assistance, and advocacy for elderly community members. Our volunteers help with daily tasks, facilitate access to services, and organize social activities that honor the wisdom and contributions of our elders.

Rebuilding After the Fire

When a devastating fire swept through Block D of Kutupalong camp in March 2023, BRSU's emergency response team was among the first to mobilize. Our volunteers helped evacuate vulnerable residents, provided first aid, and distributed emergency supplies.

In the weeks that followed, our team worked alongside affected families to clear debris, salvage belongings, and rebuild shelters. We organized a community support network that provided temporary housing, meals, and emotional support to those who lost everything.

"The BRSU volunteers gave us hope when we had lost everything. They worked day and night to help us rebuild not just our homes, but our sense of community." â€” Fatima K., fire survivor

Creating Safe Spaces for Children

Our child-friendly spaces program has created five dedicated areas where children can play, learn, and process their experiences in a safe environment. These spaces serve over 500 children weekly and have become vital community hubs.

"My son was withdrawn and fearful after our journey to Bangladesh. At the BRSU children's center, he found friends and started to smile again. The volunteers understand what our children have been through." â€” Noor, parent

Health Education Saves Lives

When a diphtheria outbreak threatened camp residents in 2022, our health advocates went door-to-door providing information about symptoms, prevention, and treatment. Their early warning system helped identify cases quickly and prevent the spread of disease.

"The BRSU health team explained the symptoms in our language and helped us get treatment quickly when my daughter fell ill. Their quick action prevented others in our family from getting sick." â€” Mohammed, father
