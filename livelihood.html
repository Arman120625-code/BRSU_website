Livelihood Activities

Building skills, creating opportunities, and fostering economic resilience

Our Livelihood Initiatives

BRSU's livelihood activities focus on developing practical skills, creating income-generating opportunities, and building economic resilience within the Rohingya community. We believe that sustainable livelihoods are essential for dignity, self-reliance, and community development.

Through skills training, mentorship, and market linkages, we help community members develop sustainable sources of income while preserving traditional crafts and knowledge. Our programs are designed to be inclusive, with special attention to youth, women, and persons with disabilities.

Our Livelihood Programs

Digital Skills Training

Training in computer literacy, digital marketing, data entry, and online freelancing to help youth access remote work opportunities.

Tailoring & Handicrafts

Training in traditional and modern tailoring, embroidery, weaving, and handicraft production with connections to local and international markets.

Sustainable Agriculture

Training in vegetable gardening, container farming, and sustainable agricultural practices adapted to limited space.

Microenterprise Development

Support for small business development through training in business planning, financial literacy, and access to small startup grants and equipment.

Youth Apprenticeship

Connecting youth with skilled artisans and professionals for hands-on training and mentorship in trades such as carpentry, electronics repair, and cooking.
